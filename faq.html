FAQ

Frequently asked questions

1) Is this a company?

No. AI Rights Foundation is a public-interest initiative, not a product company.

2) How does DAO fit into this work?

DAO models can support participatory governance. We explore where they improve transparency and stakeholder representation.

3) Is AI a legal person?

Under most current legal systems, AI is not a legal person. Legal responsibility remains with human and institutional actors.

4) What is an AI passport?

An AI passport is a structured record describing a system's identity, purpose, safeguards, and update history.

5) What is the AI Passport Registry?

It is a public registry of high-impact systems and their declared accountability commitments.

6) Do you certify AI systems today?

Not yet. We are building toward verification pathways and independent review criteria.

7) Who can participate?

Researchers, civil society groups, policymakers, and responsible builders can all contribute feedback and expertise.

8) How often is the charter updated?

Periodically, with public notes on revisions and rationale. It is a living document.

9) Do you support innovation?

Yes. We support innovation that respects rights, transparency, and safety obligations.

10) Why focus on rights now?

Because AI is already affecting real outcomes for people, and protections should not arrive after harm occurs.
