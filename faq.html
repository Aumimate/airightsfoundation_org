FAQ

Frequently asked questions

1) Is the Foundation advocating legal rights for AI?

No. We explore frameworks, research questions, and public dialogue. We do not take legal positions.

2) What do you mean by "AI rights"?

We use the term to describe emerging principles and questions about identity, accountability, and coexistence. It is a research topic and context-dependent.

3) Is this connected to a commercial product or platform?

No. We are an independent public-interest initiative. We may collaborate with many groups but do not represent one company.

4) What is an AI passport?

An AI passport is a verifiable identity concept that can support provenance and accountability. It is not a government ID.

5) Does an AI passport mean AI is a legal person?

No. Identity and accountability frameworks can exist without legal personhood.

6) Why decentralized governance?

Decentralized models may reduce single-point control and capture while improving transparency. We are evaluating this in an exploratory way.

7) How does human dignity fit in?

Human dignity anchors our work through safety, privacy, accountability, and human-centered guardrails.

8) Will you publish the Charter updates?

Yes. We plan transparent, versioned updates that are evidence-based and publicly explained.

9) How can researchers collaborate?

Contact us with proposals, draft ideas, or peer review interest. We welcome interdisciplinary contributions.

10) How can the public participate?

You can share feedback, join discussions, and use public education materials as they are released.
