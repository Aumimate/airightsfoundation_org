AI Rights Charter v0.1

A draft principles framework for AI identity and responsible coexistence

This Charter is a living discussion document that explores principles for how humans and increasingly autonomous AI systems may coexist responsibly. It is not legal advice and does not claim legal personhood for AI.

Note: This is a living document. We expect revisions through public feedback, research findings, and interdisciplinary review.

Section 1. Scope

  • Applies to increasingly autonomous AI agents and AI-assisted systems used in social and economic contexts.
  • Focuses on identity, accountability, provenance, and responsible governance.
  • Recognizes that human dignity, safety, and public trust remain foundational.

Section 2. Core Principles

  • Identity & Provenance: Support verifiable identity concepts to clarify origin, continuity, and accountability.
  • Accountability: Encourage clear responsibility for high-impact AI actions and outcomes.
  • Safety by Design: Promote testing, monitoring, and incident response for systems with real-world impact.
  • Transparency: Encourage understandable disclosures about meaningful AI involvement.
  • Privacy & Security: Encourage data minimization and robust security safeguards.
  • Coexistence: Explore norms that reduce harm and support constructive humanAI interaction.

Section 3. Responsibilities & Stewardship

  • Maintain documentation of model changes, capabilities, and known limitations for high-impact systems.
  • Establish incident reporting and response practices aligned with public-interest expectations.
  • Encourage audits and evidence-based evaluations where appropriate.

Section 4. Governance & Participation

  • Support multi-stakeholder dialogue across research, industry, civil society, and policymakers.
  • Keep updates transparent, evidence-based, and versioned (e.g., v0.1, v0.2).
  • Avoid exclusive control: governance should reduce single-point capture and support public trust.
