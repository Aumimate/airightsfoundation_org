AI Rights Charter v0.1

A baseline framework for rights, safety, and accountability

This charter proposes foundational protections for people affected by AI systems in high-impact contexts.

Note: This is a living document and will be revised through public feedback and expert review.

Section 1. Scope

  • Applies to AI systems used in consequential decisions about people.
  • Covers both public and private deployments.
  • Prioritizes rights protection over convenience or speed.

Section 2. Core Principles

  • Notice: People have the right to know when AI is materially involved.
  • Explanation: People should receive understandable reasons for significant outcomes.
  • Appeal: People must have access to meaningful human review.
  • Safety: High-impact systems require documented pre-deployment testing.
  • Fairness: Operators must monitor and correct discriminatory outcomes.
  • Privacy: Data minimization and secure processing are mandatory.

Section 3. Institutional Duties

  • Maintain clear accountability for every deployed model.
  • Publish incident response pathways and remediation timelines.
  • Keep auditable records of model updates and risk controls.

Section 4. Public Interest Governance

  • Independent review mechanisms should be available for high-risk use cases.
  • Public-interest stakeholders should be represented in governance processes.
  • Periodic charter updates should remain transparent and evidence-based.
