What we do

Five pillars guide our work across policy, research, and civic education.

1. Charter Development

Publish and improve a clear AI Rights Charter that institutions can adopt.

2. Registry Stewardship

Maintain an AI Passport Registry to track high-impact systems and declared safeguards.

3. Verification Methods

Define practical checks for transparency, appeal rights, and incident reporting.

4. Public Guidance

Create plain-language materials for communities, schools, and policymakers.

5. Multi-stakeholder Governance

Support governance models that balance technical progress with human dignity.

Why this matters

AI systems increasingly shape access to health, education, finance, and public services. When systems fail, people need clear rights, recourse, and independent oversight.

Our role is to help society move from vague promises to measurable protections.

Blue and green, in balance

AI progress should remain aligned with human rights. Our standards are designed to protect both innovation and dignity.
