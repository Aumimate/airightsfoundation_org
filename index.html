What we do

Five pillars guide our work across research, framework development, and public understanding.

1. Charter (Draft Principles)

Maintain a living principles document to support thoughtful discussion on AI identity, accountability, and responsible deployment.

2. Identity & Registry Concepts

Explore verifiable identity approaches (e.g., AI 'passport' concepts) that may help clarify provenance and accountability for high-impact systems.

3. Governance Frameworks

Study governance modelscentralized and decentralizedthat can improve oversight, transparency, and responsible coordination.

4. Public Resources

Create plain-language materials that help communities, educators, and organizations understand emerging AI identity and governance questions.

5. Multi-stakeholder Dialogue

Convene constructive dialogue among researchers, technologists, civil society, and policymakers to refine shared frameworks over time.

Why this matters

AI systems increasingly influence access to services, information, and opportunities. As AI becomes more capable, society will need clearer ways to think about identity, accountability, and long-term governance.

Our role is to contribute research and practical frameworks that support responsible integrationwithout taking specific legal positions.

Blue and green, in balance

AI innovation should advance alongside human dignity and safety. We focus on practical frameworks that help align progress with public trust.
